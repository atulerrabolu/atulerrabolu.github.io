Machine Learning Research

(Python, Keras)

• My partner and I researched whether or not a machine could predict "random" human behavior. 
The paper was named, "The Prediction of Seemingly Stochastic Human Behavior Using Neural Networks"

• We Asked users to enter a consecutive stream of 100 random numbers ranging from 1-3. We utilized an
 LSTM neural network and trained it on the data set incrementally to see whether or not it had any predictive
 power on a human's next generated number at any current point in the data stream.

• We found that the LSTM neural network was able to outperform random guessing by 39.159%
 indicating that humans are not inherently random and a computer can predict future human behavior
 on even seemingly random behavior.
